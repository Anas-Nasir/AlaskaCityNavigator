Overview of the city and population

Sitka, Alaska, located on Baranof Island in the Southeast Panhandle, is a city with a population of approximately 8,600 as of the 2020 Census. Founded as a Russian trading post in the late 18th century, Sitka played a key role in the transfer of Alaska from Russia to the United States in 1867. Today, it boasts a unique blend of Russian colonial heritage and Tlingit culture, evident in landmarks like the Russian Orthodox Cathedral and the Sitka National Historical Park.
